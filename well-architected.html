Well-Architected Framework (WAF)

Building high-quality, enterprise-grade cloud solutions through five core pillars.

The Five Pillars

Reliability Ensuring systems recover from failures and continue to function.
Security Protecting data and assets from threats and unauthorized access.
Cost Optimization Managing costs to maximize the value delivered.
Operational Excellence Running and monitoring systems to deliver business value.
Performance Efficiency Scaling to meet changes in demand effectively.

Importance of Compliance

At The Data Linq, we don't just build; we validate. WAF compliance ensures that your Data Lakehouse isn't just "running"—it's optimized for long-term sustainability. Without these checks, technical debt accumulates rapidly, leading to skyrocketing Azure bills and system instability.

Best Practices

  • Automated Reviews: Use Azure Advisor and custom scoring to check your architecture against WAF benchmarks weekly.
  • Principle of Least Privilege: Within the Security pillar, ensure identity access is scoped to the smallest possible requirement.
  • Right-Sizing: Continuously monitor compute clusters (Databricks/Synapse) to ensure you aren't over-provisioning resources.
  • Design for Failure: Implement multi-region disaster recovery to satisfy the Reliability pillar for mission-critical data.
